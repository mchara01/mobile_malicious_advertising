ANALYSIS AND IDENTIFICATION OF WIRETAPPING APPS

When a WireTapping attempt is Detected, this App tries to Identify the App Responsible for the Attack/Event.

In most cases, it gets it right.

However, there may be cases where "WireTap Removal" can make an error.

In some cases a Responsible App simply cannot be found, in some other few cases "WireTap Removal" will blame another App than the Guilty one.

This is particularly true for Apps which make Hidden Calls - which may be hard to Detect - e.g. because they are often active some time before the actual Hidden Call is taking place.

The Main Purpose of this App is to Detect WireTapping Attempts and Identify the Apps Responsible.

 So; you should be particularly careful before UNINSTALLing an App - before you are quite sure that it is the Correct App you want to get rid of.

The Identification of Apps will get more accurate and reliable after you have Whitelisted most of your own Apps - those Apps that you often use.

Once you have Whitelisted all of your Innocent Apps that generate a Warning, please Read This.

RECORDINGS

There may exist a Spy App on your Phone which makes Recordings by using the Microphone or the Camera of your Device.

The purpose is to listen in on you.

Such a method will also record VoIP Calls, such as made by Skype, etc.

Each Recording is saved in a file, which may be sent (e.g. via Email) to some unknown receiver.

If the Microphone is used - or a Video Recording is being made - on your Device, "WireTap Removal" will detect it.

This also means that - if you are going to use the Microphone yourself, you should first Disable "WireTap Removal" - to avoid a Warning.

Please note that it is only possible to detect a  Recording at Regular Intervals - as set by you (on the Settings Page).

A short Checking Period will increase the chances of detecting brief Recordings, but will increase Battery Consumption slightly.

BEFORE MAKING A RECORDING - OR A VoIP CALL - YOU SHOULD DISABLE THIS APP

Before you are making one of the following, you may want to DISABLE "WireTap Removal":

- A Voice/Audio Recording,
- A Video Recording,
- A Skype Call,
- A VoIP Call, etc.

All of these use the Microphone of your Device, which will be detected by "WireTap Removal" as a Recording.

If you do NOT disable "WireTap Removal", your Recording/VoIP Call will be registered by two Log entries; One when you Started the Recording/Call, and  a Second when it was ended (at the times when the Mic Checking was done, as specified by you).

An even better Alternative - when this App detects that you start the Recording/Call - is to WHITELIST the app.

Then it should not be detected the next time you are using it.

(Normal Phone Calls do NOT use the normal Microphone of your Device, so you do not have to Disable this App before making Normal Phone Calls.)

FALSE DETECTION OF RECORDINGS?

False Detection of Recordings should NEVER occur on Android 5 and older.

On Android 6, False Detection of Recordings may occur.

If you see a Call Recording or a Microphone Recording being Detected, it is very likely caused by a REAL Recording.

Either done by a Spy App, by yourself (running your own Recording App), or by Another App using the Microphone.

On Android 6, If you get a Warning about a Recording by an app that you know, you should Whitelist it.

CHECKING PERIOD

The Checking of Recordings is only made at Regular Intervals - selected by you (on the Settings page) - e.g. once per minute.

If a Recording App is running in a shorter time than the selected Check Period/Interval, it cannot be detected.

Also - when you are testing to check whether this App will Detect your Recording - you must also Stop for long enough to make this App Register that the recording has in fact Stopped, before it will Detect a New Recording.

Setting a very low Check Period will increase the Battery Consumption.

Another reason for Not Detecting a Recording, is that the App that was doing it, has been Whitelisted by you. E.g. if the "Google Play Services" has been Whitelisted, Recordings that are done by that App - or other Apps that is using the "Google Play Services" to do Recordings for them - the Recording will Not be Registered.

If you want to be able to detect your manually started Recordings better/quicker, you should reduce the Checking Period. But please note that this will increase Battery Consumption.

If you agree that the Main Purpose of this App is to Detect other than your own (i.e. possibly Spy) Apps, you should be able to live with a relatively long Checking Period.

You should only Uninstall (6) an app after having made sure that it really is a Spy App.

IDENTIFYING APPS THAT READ YOUR LOCATION

Identifying Apps that read your Location is a rather tricky matter (for this App).

Facts:

1. Some Apps that read your Location only run for a very short time.

2. Other Apps that read your Location run almost continuously - hidden in the background - but they never stop or start. They just keep on running, occasionally reading your Location.

3. When you are running one of your own Apps which reads the Location (e.g. such as a GPS Tester, a Map or Navigational App, a Speedometer, etc.), they may read the Location very often, and more or less set up a "Screen" or "Shield" - through which other Location Reading apps can hardly penetrate.

Hence; In order to be able to catch - and identify an App that is reading you Location - you should:

- Set up a very Short Checking Period (on the Settings page) (But please note that you should not continue doing this for a long time, as it will increase the battery consumption considerably.)

- Avoid using Location Reading Apps of your own (e.g. such as GPS Testers, Map or Navigational Apps, Speedometers, etc.).

In short: When you are running one of your Own Location Reading Apps, you should not expect to be able to catch and Identify a Real WireTapping Location Reading App - so you may as well DISABLE this App.

HIDDEN CALLS

Hidden Phone Calls may be set up (i.e. connected) controlled remotely from another Device, but done by a Spy App on your Phone.
The purpose is to listen in on you - without you noticing or hearing it.

There are two types of Hidden Calls; Outgoing and Incoming.

An Outgoing Hidden Call is made from your Phone, by (an App) calling another number.

An Incoming Hidden Call is made from another phone, making a Call to your Phone, but after the Ringer has been set to Silent on your Phone (by a Spy App).

Both Outgoing and Incoming Hidden Calls are silent (so that you will not hear anything), but the usual Call Screen is normally shown.

If Hidden Calls are registered on your Phone, you may also want to consult our free app "Anti Spy" - and compare the Responsible App suggested by "WireTap Removal" by those listed by "Anti Spy" in order to find the Spy App which seems to be causing the trouble - and remove it from your Device.

If you suspect that some of the registered Hidden Calls are caused by FALSE Detections, please read below.

"WIRETAP REMOVAL" is NOT a Spy App!

An AntiSpy App lists this App as being a "Suspicious App".

Based on the Permissions this App needs, it says that "WireTap Removal":

"Can Read your Location",
"Records Audio on Voice Calls",
"Handles Outgoing Calls",
 "Runs on device startup".

Except for Starting at Startup, this App does not do any of these things - even if it has the permissions to do so.

As shown on its Description on the Google Play Store, this App needs the following Permissions:

ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION,
ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION,
RECORD_AUDIO,
PROCESS_OUTGOING_CALLS (sometimes called REROUTING_OUTGOING_CALLS),
plus a few others of less interest in this connection.

The ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION and ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION Permissions are needed in order to check whether ANOTHER App is Reading your Location, using the GPS or NETWORK, respectively.

The RECORD_AUDIO Permission is used by this App to check if some other App is using the Microphone. It NEVER makes any Recording.

The PROCESS_OUTGOING_CALLS Permission is used by this App to receive a notification each time there is an Outgoing Phone Call, and to get the Outgoing Call Phone Number. It NEVER "processes" the Call, or affects it in any way.

Hence; "WireTap Removal" is NOT A SPY APP. On the contrary, it tries to DETECT when you are being spied on!

SKIPPING WHITELISTED APPS IN APPS ANALYZES

In the normal analysis of Apps which are considered (to be Responsible for a possible WireTap Attack), all relevant Apps are taken into consideration. And the most likely app is picked. Even if it is a Whitelisted App.  (But if it is a Whitelisted App, it will - of course - not generate a Warning.)

After you have used this App for a while, you will probably have Whitelisted all of the Apps that you normally use. So that you should no longer be bothered by the Red Warning Screen.

Now you are probably only interested in getting Warnings on REAL WireTapping Attempts.

In order to better achieve this goal, we have introduced a Setting that will Change the Analysis Algorithms.

By enabling "Skip Whitelisted Apps in Analyzes" (on the Settings page), the Analysis Algorithms of Apps will try to SKIP (Ignore) Whitelisted Apps, in order to better Concentrate on the Non-Whitelisted Apps. During this Process, a Whitelisted App which may be the most likely App to be picked, will be Ignored, while the other (Non-Whitelisted Apps) will be Analyzed in more depth.

(But if the Whitelisted App is the only possible Culprit found, IT will still be picked - but giving no Warning.)

A negative side-effect of this method is that some Innocent App may be Identified as Responsible for a WireTapping Attempt (e.g. while the Real Culprit was one of the Whitelisted Apps.). However, as you should have Whitelisted all of your Apps that can cause a Warning by now, this should not happen often.

A positive effect of this method is to Detect ANY NEW App that can WireTap your Device.

It will also increase the likelihood of Identifying Spy Apps that are well hidden, and/or designed to avoid Detection.

You should NOT Enable this Setting before you consider yourself finished with Whitelisting all of your own (Innocent) Apps which can cause a Warning.

WHAT DOES "SYSTEM OVERLOAD" STAND FOR?

When this Error Message is shown in the Log, there is a System Overload situation in your Device. It means that there are too many Apps running, but too little Memory available. In order to free the required Memory, the Android Operating System has to Stop several Apps, even those that have a High Priority, like this App.

When this happens, this App may no longer function properly. In particular, the Detection of Hidden Calls may Fail.

You can optionally Enable (or Disable) "Automatic Disable Detection of Hidden Calls at Overload" at the Settings page.

If this Option is Enabled, the Detection of Hidden Calls will automatically be Disabled when a System Overload occurs. The purpose of this is to avoid further False Detections of Hidden Calls. If you want to continue Detection of Hidden Calls even if System Overloads occurs on your Device, you should Disable this Setting.

If the Detection of Hidden Calls has been Automatically Disabled in this way, you must manually reEnable it - if you want to continue Detecting Hidden Calls.

 If you see the System Overload Event in the Log - and you want to continue Detecting Hidden Calls - you should Stop or Uninstall all the Apps that you do not use or need. A restart of your Device may also help temporarily.

If you continue seeing the System Overload Event in your log, this App cannot reliably Detect Hidden Calls, so you should DISABLE it (in order to avoid seeing all the False Hidden Calls Events in the Log).

I HAVE DISABLED MICROPHONE AND LOCATION PERMISSIONS FOR AN APP, BUT IT STILL ALERTS ME. WHY?

This may happen if your device is using Android version 6 ("Marshmallows") or later/newer.

It should not happen for Apps that have been targeted for Android version 6 or later, but it may happen for Apps that have been targeted for older versions of Android (e.g. version 5 and older).

If you attempt to disable/reject a Permission for an older App, you will be warned (by Android) that if you reject that Permission, the App may no longer work properly.

So; If you do reject/disable a Permission for an older App in this way, you have no guarantee that this app will continue working as expected.

So; If you are alerted by an App that seems to be making a Recording or Reading you Location - and it is an older App - it is normally of no use to try to remove/disable the relevant Permissions. It may not work.

So; If you are alerted by an older App, you should either WHITELIST it (if you trust the App), or UNINSTALL it.

ABOUT RUNTIME PERMISSIONS

For Android 6 and later, the User must grant certain "dangerous" Permissions for an App.

For this App, these are Permissions to RECORD_AUDIO, READ_PHONE_STATE and ACCESS_LOCATION..

The first is required in order to be able to check the Microphone of your Device - if it is being used or not.

The second is required to be able to get Information about the Phone State - e.g. when it is in a Call State, read the Phone Number you are Calling, etc.

The third is required in order to be able to read the Location of your Device.

The app will only work if you Grant all of these Permissions.

If you do not want to Grant these Permissions, the App cannot enter the Enabled state, and thus is useless.

Permissions for an App can also be granted - or rejected - from the Device's Settings App.

If you reject a Permission in that way - that you have previously granted - the app may misbehave, or stop.

In order to get it working properly again, you must Restart and/or Disable it. It will then ask for that Permission again.

If you have clicked on the Option to 'Never ask again' - the Request for Permissions will not be shown again.

If you do want to have it shown again - after all - you must uninstall the app, and reinstall it.

If you wonder why the App needs these Permissions, please read this.
